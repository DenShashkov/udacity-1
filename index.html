Lanterns of Hoi An
on May 28, 2020

Our next stop in Vietnam was little town of Hoi An, situated right in the middle of it. If asked «Where you'd like to come back one day?», I'd pick Hoi An without any doubt.. Old Town Hội An, the city's historic district, is recognized as an exceptionally well-preserved example of a Southeast Asian trading port dating from the 15th to the 19th century, its buildings and street plan reflecting a blend of indigenous and foreign influences.

Nature taking over in Angkor
on May 33, 2020

Ancient temples of Angkor Vat in Cambodia were abandoned for centuries and jungle is taking over. Now rediscovered for the world this temple complex in nature park gives us unique opportunity to witness nature's overtaking these temples built from stone between 12th and 13th century. It is the largest religious monument in the world and i can't use any other word than «breathtaking», trying to describe our emotions there

Sunset in Saigon
on May 35, 2020

City of Saigon is now officially known as Ho Chi Mingh City, but even locals quite often continue to call it Saigon. Once a capital of Southern Vietnam, torn apart by civil war now is the most populous city in Vietnam with a population of 13+ million people. It is a buzzing loud city located on both sides of Saigon River. The city's center has some wide American-style boulevards and a few French colonial buildings.
